1. **Centers of Influence (COIs):** Build referral partnerships with CPAs and attorneys who already serve physician clients. Medical practice accountants are the #1 warm intro source.
2. **LinkedIn:** Physicians are increasingly active. Target practice owners (not employed docs). Engage with posts about practice management, burnout, and financial topics.
3. **Educational Seminars/Webinars:** Host a "Tax Strategies for Practice Owners" dinner or CE-adjacent event at a venue near Cleveland Clinic or Akron campuses.
4. **Direct Mail:** Physicians still read physical mail — particularly high-quality, personalized letters (not mass flyers). Target practice addresses.
5. **Association Sponsorships:** Sponsor AMCNO or OSMA events to get face time with physician leaders.

### Pain Points Aligned with Bryan's Offering
- **Massive tax burden:** Physicians earning $300K-$800K+ face 37%+ federal marginal rates. They start earning late (after residency, age 30-35) and feel urgency to catch up.
- **Student loan debt vs. wealth building:** Many carry $200K-$400K in loans while needing to simultaneously fund retirement.
- **Practice sale/succession:** Solo and group practice owners nearing retirement need to convert illiquid practice value into reliable income streams (annuity fit).
- **Malpractice and liability exposure:** Asset protection through insurance vehicles is highly relevant.
- **Hidden fees in retirement accounts:** Physician 401(k)s from hospital systems are often loaded with high-fee mutual funds — a direct fit for Bryan's fee-elimination message.
- **Cash balance plan opportunities:** Practice owners can shelter $100K-$300K+ annually in tax-deductible contributions through defined benefit/cash balance plans.

### Best Messaging Angle
> *"Most physicians I work with are shocked when they see how much of their retirement savings is being eroded by fees they didn't know existed — and how much more they're paying in taxes than necessary. I help practice owners in Northeast Ohio keep more of what they earn using insurance-based strategies that eliminate portfolio fees, reduce tax exposure, and provide guaranteed retirement income. Would a 15-minute review be worth your time?"*

**Hook:** "You survived medical school and built a practice — but your financial plan might be costing you $50K+ a year in hidden fees and unnecessary taxes."

## CROSS-INDUSTRY PROSPECTING STRATEGY SUMMARY

### Universal Data Sources (All 7 Industries)
| Platform | Cost | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Data Axle / Salesgenie** | ~$150-300/mo | Best SMB coverage for local businesses. Filter by SIC code, geography, revenue, owner name. Industry-leading for non-tech businesses. |
| **Apollo.io** | $50-150/mo | Verified emails and direct dials. Good for professional services. |
| **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** | ~$100/mo | Filter by title, industry, geography. Best for professionals (doctors, lawyers, dealers). |
| **Ohio Secretary of State** | Free | Business entity filings — identify LLC/Corp owners by name. |
| **eLicense Ohio** | Free | License lookup for regulated professions (doctors, dentists, lawyers). |
| **Google Maps + Manual Research** | Free | For small universes (powersports, promo companies). |

### Recommended Priority Ranking (by ease of access + alignment with Bryan's offering)
1. **Dentists** — Tight-knit community, clear pain points, accessible through study clubs and dental societies. Highest conversion potential.
2. **Doctors** — Large market, high income, massive tax burden. Slightly harder to access than dentists but enormous upside.
3. **Lawyers** — Analytical buyers who appreciate the fee-elimination pitch. Accessible through bar association events.
4. **Car Dealerships** — Huge wealth, complex tax situations, active local associations (GCADA, NOADA). Relationship-driven sale.
5. **Trucking Companies** — Large universe, clear insurance needs, underserved market. Tax and retirement messaging resonates.
6. **Powersports Dealers** — Small universe but aging owners with urgent succession needs. DealerConnect 2026 in Columbus is a must-attend.
7. **Apparel/Promo Companies** — Smallest and least wealthy of the 7, but underserved and accessible. OPPA events are the entry point.

### Daily Prospecting Cadence (Recommended)
- **30 min:** LinkedIn engagement (comment on 10-15 posts from target prospects)
- **30 min:** Personalized outreach (5-10 connection requests or emails referencing specific pain points)
- **2 hours:** Protected phone block (50-75 dials → expect 8-12 conversations → 2-3 appointments)
- **Ongoing:** COI relationship building (CPAs, attorneys, equipment reps, practice brokers)
- **Monthly:** 1-2 educational events (dinner seminars, webinar, study club presentations)
