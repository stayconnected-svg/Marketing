# PLAYBOOK 1: DENTISTS (Priority #1)

---

## 1. Industry Overview

Dentistry is the highest-priority vertical for Bryan's practice. Approximately 10,000 dentists retire annually in the U.S., and the majority have 60–80% of their net worth locked inside their practice. These are high-income earners ($250K–$600K) who are typically underserved by generalist financial advisors and face unique challenges around practice valuation, succession planning, and the looming 2026 tax law changes (TCJA sunset). Northeast Ohio has a dense concentration of private-practice dentists accessible through well-organized professional associations and study clubs.

---

## 2. Pain Points to Reference

Use these in conversations, emails, and presentations:

- **Concentrated wealth risk** — 60–80% of net worth tied up in a single illiquid asset (their practice)
- **Succession planning gap** — Most have no formal exit strategy; practice worth $500K–$2M but no plan to minimize tax on sale
- **Tax exposure on sale** — Could lose 30–40% of practice value to federal and state taxes without proper structuring
- **2026 TCJA sunset** — Current favorable tax rates expire; without action, rates revert to higher pre-2017 levels
- **Fee erosion** — Hidden fees in 401(k) plans and advisory accounts quietly draining retirement savings
- **Income without a safety net** — High earnings but often no pension, no deferred comp, and limited tax-advantaged savings beyond basic retirement plans
- **Staff retention costs** — Struggling to offer competitive benefits without sophisticated plan design
- **Disability/income risk** — One hand injury can end a career; most are underinsured for specialty disability

# PLAYBOOK 2: DOCTORS / PHYSICIANS (Priority #2)

---

## 1. Industry Overview

Physicians represent Bryan's second-priority vertical. They earn $200K–$600K+ annually but face punishing marginal tax rates (37%+), carry significant student debt ($200K–$400K), and are chronically underserved by financial advisors who understand their unique compensation structures. Northeast Ohio's concentration of major health systems (Cleveland Clinic, University Hospitals, Akron General, Summa Health) creates a dense market of both employed and private-practice physicians. The best entry point is through CPA referrals and educational dinner seminars positioned near major medical campuses.

---

## 2. Pain Points to Reference

Use these in conversations, emails, and presentations:

- **Extreme marginal tax rates** — 37%+ federal, plus 4.8% Ohio state; effective rates often exceed 45% with NIIT and Medicare surtax
- **Massive student debt** — $200K–$400K in loans; many still paying into their 40s and 50s
- **Hidden 401(k)/403(b) fees** — Hospital-sponsored plans often have expensive fund options and administrative fees that go unexamined
- **Cash balance plan ignorance** — Most private-practice physicians don't know they can shelter $100K–$300K+ annually in tax-deductible contributions
- **No pension replacement** — Unlike previous generations, today's physicians have no defined-benefit safety net
- **Practice succession uncertainty** — Private-practice doctors face the same concentrated-wealth problem as dentists
- **Burnout-driven early retirement** — Many want to retire at 55–60 but haven't planned for the income gap before Social Security/Medicare
- **Malpractice and liability exposure** — Asset protection is a constant concern

# PLAYBOOK 3: LAWYERS / ATTORNEYS (Priority #3)

---

## 1. Industry Overview

Attorneys are Bryan's third-priority vertical. Northeast Ohio has a massive legal community anchored by the Cleveland Metropolitan Bar Association (~5,000 members), large law firms downtown, and hundreds of small/solo practitioners across Summit, Portage, and surrounding counties. Attorneys face unique financial challenges including feast-or-famine contingency income, partnership buy-in/buy-out complexity, and some of the highest effective tax rates of any profession (often 50%+ when combining federal, state, self-employment, and local taxes). They are analytically minded, skeptical of sales pitches, and respond best to data-driven, peer-referred introductions. The best entry points are CPA referral networks, estate attorney reciprocal relationships, and CMBA events.

---

## 2. Pain Points to Reference

Use these in conversations, emails, and presentations:

- **Extreme tax burden** — 50%+ effective rates when combining federal (37%), Ohio (4.8%), municipal (2–3%), self-employment tax, and NIIT
- **Feast-or-famine income** — Contingency-fee attorneys have wildly variable annual income; hard to plan retirement savings
- **No pension or deferred comp** — Unlike judges or government attorneys, private-practice lawyers have no safety net
- **Hidden advisory fees** — Most don't scrutinize their own financial plans the way they'd scrutinize a contract
- **Partnership buy-sell funding gaps** — Many firms have unfunded or underfunded buy-sell agreements
- **Structured fee deferrals** — Opportunities to defer large contingency fees into tax-advantaged structures are often missed
- **Burnout and late-career transitions** — High rates of mid-career exits; need income bridge strategies
- **Estate planning for themselves** — Ironically, many estate attorneys haven't optimized their own financial plans
