**Why these questions matter:**

- If **templates can be pre-approved for reuse**, we build a template library (20-30 templates), get them all approved in one batch, and then our automation can fire at will using those templates with personalized fill-ins. This is the best-case scenario.

- If **insurance-only content is separate**, we may be able to split outreach into two streams: insurance-focused (faster, lighter regulation) and securities-focused (requires LPL approval).
- **Turnaround time** tells us how to batch. If it's 24-48 hours, we can work with that. If it's 2-3 weeks, we need to pre-approve everything in large batches upfront.

**Best case scenario:** Templates are pre-approvable + 1-to-1 correspondence is lighter + non-securities content is exempt. This gives us a fast lane for most outreach.

**Worst case scenario:** Every individual message needs pre-approval regardless of content. In this case, we front-load a massive batch of templates and get them all approved before launching.
