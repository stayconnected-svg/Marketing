Types of Annuities

Fixed Annuities

Fixed annuities offer a predictable income stream, appealing to those seeking stability. We help you understand how these annuities work, including their interest rates and payout structures. This knowledge enables you to determine if a fixed annuity aligns with your financial security needs.

*Fixed annuities are long-term investment vehicles designed for retirement purposes. Gains from tax-deferred investments are taxable as ordinary income upon withdrawal. Guarantees are based on the claims paying ability of the issuing company. Withdrawals made prior to age 59 ½ are subject to a 10% IRS penalty tax and surrender charges may apply.

Income Annuities

For those prioritizing a steady income in retirement, income annuities might be an option. We explain how these annuities provide regular payments, ensuring you grasp their benefits and limitations. Our guidance aids in evaluating if an income annuity fits your retirement income strategy.

Variable Annuities

Variable annuities present opportunities for growth through market exposure, but they also come with risks. We break down the complexities of these annuities, including investment options, income riders, and legacy options. Our insights help you weigh the pros and cons, making it easier to decide if a variable annuity suits your risk tolerance and objectives.

*Variable Annuities are suitable for long-term investing, such as retirement investing. Withdrawals prior to age 59 ½ may be subject to tax penalties and surrender charges may apply. Variable annuities are subject to market risk and may lose value.
