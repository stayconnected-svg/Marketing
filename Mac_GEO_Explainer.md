## Your Google Visibility Score: 8 out of 100

In the ranking report, we scored your site at **8/100** compared to your competitors. Here's exactly what goes into that number and why yours is where it is.

We look at five factors — the same things Google uses to decide who shows up first:

### Factor 1: Keyword Rankings (How many searches find you)

This is the most straightforward one. We tested 12 search terms your ideal clients would actually type — things like "annuity advisor hudson ohio" or "should I keep my annuity" — and checked whether your site appears on the first three pages of results.

**Your score: 0 keywords ranking on page 1.** The only search that finds you is someone typing your exact company name. That means zero discovery — no one new is finding you through Google.

### Factor 2: Domain Authority (How much Google trusts your site)

Domain authority is a measure of how "important" Google considers your website overall. It's built up over time through other reputable sites linking to you, age of your domain, and consistent publishing.

Big sites like Bankrate or UBS.com have high authority. A brand-new or template-based site starts near the bottom.

**Your score: Very low.** Your site is built on the FMG template platform — Google sees thousands of nearly identical FMG sites. There's nothing telling Google yours is more important than any other.

### Factor 3: Local SEO Signals (Does Google know where you are and who you serve)

When someone searches "annuity advisor near me" or includes a city name, Google looks for signals that your business is real, local, and active in that area. This includes your Google Business Profile, directory listings (Yelp, Bing Places, Apple Maps), and whether your name/address/phone are consistent everywhere.

**Your score: Minimal.** You have a couple of directory listings (MapQuest, CitySquares), but no optimized Google Business Profile and no consistent presence across the major directories. Google doesn't confidently associate you with Hudson/Akron/Cleveland searches.

### Factor 4: Content Quality (Do you answer the questions people are asking)

Google's AI needs content to pull from. It looks for pages that go deep on a topic, answer specific questions clearly, and demonstrate genuine expertise. A generic "services" page doesn't count — Google wants articles, guides, and educational content written by someone who clearly knows the subject.

**Your score: Zero unique content.** Your current FMG template pages say "we offer financial planning" in the same language thousands of other advisors use. There's nothing for Google to index that's different from anyone else. No blog posts, no educational articles, no FAQ pages, no guides.

### Factor 5: Backlink Profile (Are other sites pointing to you as a resource)

When another website links to yours, Google treats it like a vote of confidence. The more quality sites that link to you, the more Google trusts you. Think of it like referrals — if respected sites send people your way, Google assumes you're worth recommending.

**Your score: Almost nothing.** You have a couple of basic directory listings, but no professional organizations, local business groups, news sites, or financial publications linking to your content. Compare that to a competitor like Nye Wealth, who has links from local organizations, industry directories, and content partnerships.
