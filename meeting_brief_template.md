# Meeting Brief Template

This is what Mac receives with every calendar invite. Short, useful, zero fluff.

---

## Format

```
MEETING WITH: [Full Name], [Credential]
PRACTICE: [Practice/Firm Name] — [City, OH]
TYPE: [Dentist / Doctor / Attorney / etc.]

CONTEXT: [1-2 sentences on why they're interested. What did they respond to? What's their likely pain point?]

OPENER: "[A natural conversation starter Mac can use in the first 30 seconds]"
```

---

## Examples

### Dentist Example

```
MEETING WITH: Dr. Craig Duhaime, D.D.S., M.S.D.
PRACTICE: Endodontic Associates — Macedonia, OH
TYPE: Dentist (Endodontics)

CONTEXT: Responded to an email about hidden fees in retirement accounts. Has been practicing 20+ years, likely thinking about succession timing.

OPENER: "Dr. Duhaime, thanks for making time. You mentioned being curious about what your retirement accounts are actually costing you — what prompted that?"
```

### Doctor Example

```
MEETING WITH: Dr. Anil Patel, M.D.
PRACTICE: Northeast Ohio Cardiology — Akron, OH
TYPE: Physician (Cardiology, Private Practice)

CONTEXT: Connected on LinkedIn, replied to a follow-up message about cash balance plans. High earner, likely in the 37%+ bracket.

OPENER: "Dr. Patel, appreciate you connecting. You mentioned cash balance plans caught your eye — has anyone walked you through how those work for someone in your position?"
```

### Attorney Example

```
MEETING WITH: James Kowalski, Esq.
PRACTICE: Kowalski & Partners LLP — Cleveland, OH
TYPE: Attorney (Personal Injury, Named Partner)

CONTEXT: Responded to the whitepaper email about hidden portfolio fees. Contingency-fee practice with variable income — structured fee deferrals likely relevant.

OPENER: "James, thanks for taking this. You deal with contracts all day — I'm guessing when you saw what's buried in the fine print of most retirement accounts, it hit a nerve?"
```

---

## Rules

1. **Under 10 lines total.** Mac should read this in 30 seconds.
2. **No marketing language.** This isn't a campaign summary. It's a cheat sheet for a conversation.
3. **The opener should sound like something Mac would actually say.** Natural, not scripted.
4. **Include the one thing that made this person raise their hand.** That's Mac's entry point into the conversation.
5. **Attach this to the calendar invite body**, not as a separate document.
