# Detailed Breakdown: The 5 Things We Do to Improve Your GEO

## 1. Content — Answer the Questions People Are Actually Googling

**The problem:** Your site has zero unique content. The FMG template pages say the same thing as thousands of other advisor sites. Google has nothing to rank you for.

**What we do:**

- Write 5 cornerstone articles targeting your highest-value keywords:
  - "Should I Keep My Variable Annuity?" (~880 searches/month)
  - "Hidden Fees Inside Annuity Contracts" (~1,200 searches/month)
  - "How to Read Your Annuity Statement"
  - "Annuity Surrender: When It Makes Sense"
  - "What a Fee Analysis Actually Reveals"
- Each article directly answers a real question in a clear, quotable way — structured so Google's AI can grab a sentence and use it as the answer
- Written in your voice, from your perspective (20 years as a wholesaler), with your credentials front and center
- Ongoing: 2–4 new articles per month to build topical depth

**Why it works:** Every article is a new page Google can index and rank. More pages targeting more keywords = more ways for prospects to find you.

---

## 2. Local Signals — Make Google Connect You to NE Ohio

**The problem:** Google doesn't confidently associate your site with Hudson, Akron, or Cleveland area searches. When someone searches "annuity advisor near me," Google doesn't know you're relevant.

**What we do:**

- **Google Business Profile:** Claim, verify, and fully optimize — photos, services, hours, description with keywords, posts, Q&A section
- **Directory submissions:** Consistent name/address/phone across 20+ platforms (Yelp, Bing Places, Apple Maps, YellowPages, BBB, industry-specific directories)
- **Location content:** Every page mentions your service area naturally — Hudson, Stow, Akron, Cleveland, NE Ohio
- **Google Maps presence:** Show up in the local 3-pack (the map results that appear before organic links)
- **Review strategy:** Framework for generating Google reviews from clients (compliant with LPL guidelines)

**Why it works:** Local signals are the fastest win. Google Business Profile alone can put you on page 1 for local searches within 30 days.

---

## 3. Structured Data — Tell Google Exactly What You Are

**The problem:** Google's AI has to guess what your business does based on page text. If your pages are generic, the AI categorizes you as "just another financial advisor" rather than "the annuity specialist in Hudson, OH."

**What we do:**

- Add schema markup (invisible code) that explicitly tells Google:
  - Business type: Financial Service / Insurance Agency
  - Specialization: Annuity education, fee analysis
  - Location: Hudson, OH with service area covering NE Ohio
  - Credentials: Series 7, Series 66, 20 years experience
  - Services offered: Annuity review, fee analysis, retirement income planning
  - Contact info, hours, accepted client types
- Add FAQ schema to article pages (makes your answers eligible for featured snippets and AI citations)
- Add article schema with author credentials (tells Google who wrote it and why they're qualified)

**Why it works:** This is like filling out a detailed form that only Google reads. Instead of guessing, Google knows exactly what you do, where, and for whom. It's the difference between a business card and a full dossier.

---

## 4. Authority — Prove You're the Expert, Not a Generalist

**The problem:** Your competitors rank because Google sees signals that they're credible (blog content, credentials displayed, years in business, associations). Your current site gives Google no reason to consider you an authority on annuities.

**What we do:**

- **Credentials prominently displayed:** Series 7, Series 66, 20 years as a wholesaler across 8 states — on every page, not buried in a bio
- **Specialization messaging:** Every page reinforces that you are annuity-focused, not a generalist. Google rewards depth over breadth.
- **Author authority:** Every article includes an author box with your background, making it clear a real expert wrote this — not a content farm
- **Topical depth:** Instead of one "annuity services" page, you have 10+ pages covering every angle. This tells Google you own this topic.
- **Experience signals:** "After reviewing thousands of annuity contracts across 8 states..." — real-world expertise Google's AI prioritizes (E-E-A-T: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)

**Why it works:** Google's AI is specifically designed to prioritize content from people with demonstrated first-hand experience. Your 20 years as a wholesaler is a massive advantage — we just need to make it visible to Google, not just to humans.

---

## 5. Backlinks — Get Other Sites Pointing to You

**The problem:** When no one links to your site, Google assumes you're not important enough to recommend. Your competitors have links from local organizations, industry directories, and content partnerships. You have two low-quality directory listings.

**What we do:**

- **Financial advisor directories:** Get listed on SmartAsset, WealthmanagementAdvisor.com, AdvisoryHQ, NAPFA (where applicable), BrightScope
- **Local business presence:** Hudson Chamber of Commerce, Akron/Cleveland business directories, NE Ohio networking organizations
- **Industry resources:** Insurance and annuity industry directories, NAFA, relevant professional associations
- **Content-earned links:** Publish content good enough that others reference it naturally (the whitepaper, the fee calculator concept, educational guides)
- **Local PR opportunities:** Guest posts or quotes in local business publications, NE Ohio financial planning roundups

**Why it works:** Every quality link is a vote of confidence in Google's eyes. You don't need hundreds — even 15–20 relevant, authoritative links will dramatically move the needle when your competitors only have 30–50 themselves.
