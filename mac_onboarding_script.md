# Mac Onboarding Conversation

**Who runs this:** Sam (or Jacob)
**Target length:** 10-15 minutes
**Goal:** Learn what Mac wants, agree on the basics, get out of his way

---

## Before the Call

Have ready:

- Calendar app open (to confirm his availability preferences)
- This script (don't read it verbatim -- just hit every question)

Do NOT bring:

- Playbooks, strategy decks, prospect lists
- Anything that requires Mac to review or approve
- Marketing jargon

---

## Opening (30 seconds)

> "Hey Mac -- quick call. We want to make sure we're set up to get you meetings without you having to think about marketing at all. I just have a few questions so we build this around what works for you."

That's it. Don't explain the system, don't pitch the approach. Go straight to questions.

---

## The Questions

Ask these in order. Write down his answers exactly. Don't elaborate or explain why you're asking unless he asks.

### 1. Ideal Client

> "Who's your favorite type of client to work with? Dentists, doctors, lawyers -- or is there a specific type of person where the conversation just clicks?"

*Let him talk. Don't steer. If he says "all of them" narrow it down:*

> "If we could only fill your calendar with one type this month, who would it be?"

### 2. Geography

> "Are we keeping this to Northeast Ohio, or are you open to working with people further out?"

### 3. Meeting Capacity

> "How many new meetings per week feels right? We don't want to overwhelm your schedule."

*If he's unsure:*

> "Would 2-3 per week be a good starting point? We can always dial up."

### 4. Scheduling

> "What days and times work best for these meetings? Any days that are off-limits?"

### 5. Booking Method

> "Do you have a Calendly or booking link you use, or do you prefer we just put things directly on your calendar? We can set one up for you if that's easier."

### 6. How He Wants to Hear From Us

> "When we book a meeting for you, how do you want to know about it? Calendar invite, text, email -- what works?"

*Also:*

> "Other than meetings, do you want updates from us or would you rather we just keep things running and you see the results on your calendar?"

### 7. Dealbreakers

> "Anyone you don't want us reaching out to? Existing clients, specific firms, people you've already talked to?"

### 8. Outreach Comfort

> "We'll be reaching out on your behalf through email and LinkedIn -- professional, educational tone, nothing spammy. Anything you'd want us to avoid saying, or are you good with us handling the messaging?"

*This is the one place where Mac might have opinions. If he wants to see a sample, show him ONE example (Template 1-A from outreach_templates.md). Don't show the whole library.*

---

## Close (30 seconds)

> "That's everything we need. We're going to get this rolling and you'll start seeing meetings show up. If a lead ever doesn't feel right, just let us know -- thumbs up or thumbs down after the call and we'll tune it."

Then:

> "Anything else on your end?"

*If no:*

> "Perfect. We'll take it from here."

## Rules for This Conversation

1. **Don't explain how anything works.** If Mac asks, keep it to one sentence: "We handle the outreach, qualify the interest, and book the meeting."
2. **Don't use the words:** sequence, automation, pipeline, campaign, funnel, leads (say "meetings" instead), CRM, drip, cadence
3. **Don't ask Mac to do anything** other than answer these questions and show up to meetings later
4. **Match his energy.** If he wants to chat, chat. If he wants to get off the phone in 5 minutes, let him.
5. **The goal is for Mac to leave this call feeling like he just handed off a weight.** Not like he signed up for a project.
