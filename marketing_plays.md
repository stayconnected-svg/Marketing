**These rules apply to every play below. If a play can't be run within these rules, we don't run it.**

## The Plays

Each play is a different **marketing message** -- a different reason to reach out and a different thing we're offering. We can run multiple plays simultaneously across different channels, or test one at a time.

---

### Play 1: The Cold Intro

**Message:** "You have a problem you don't know about. Let's talk."

**What it looks like:**
- Short email or LinkedIn message introducing Mac's expertise
- Names the specific pain point for that vertical (hidden fees, tax exposure, no exit plan)
- Asks for 15 minutes

**Lead-in offer:** A conversation. Nothing else.

**Best for:** Prospects who are action-oriented, don't want to read anything, just want to talk to someone who knows their situation.

**Existing assets:** Templates 1-A, 2-A, 3-A in `outreach_templates.md`. Simpl ABI sequence Email 1 (The Hook) in `simpl_abi_outreach_sequence.md`.

**Automatable:** Yes -- this is the simplest sequence to automate. Cold email + follow-up on a schedule.

**Risk level:** Lowest effort, highest volume, lowest conversion per send. But it's a numbers game and it scales.

---

### Play 2: The Whitepaper Lead Magnet

**Message:** "Here's something worth reading before we talk."

**What it looks like:**
- Email offering the whitepaper as a free download
- Positions Mac as an expert without asking for anything
- Follow-up email to anyone who clicks/downloads: "Did you get a chance to read it? Happy to walk you through the key points."

**Lead-in offer:** Free educational content. No commitment.

**Best for:** Prospects who are analytical, skeptical of sales pitches, want to evaluate before engaging. Lawyers especially respond well to this.

**Existing assets:** Templates 1-B, 2-B, 3-B in `outreach_templates.md`. Simpl ABI sequence Email 2 (The Value Add) links to the whitepaper. checkannuity.com hosts the download.

**Automatable:** Yes -- email with link, track clicks/downloads, auto-follow-up with anyone who engaged.

**Risk level:** Slightly lower volume but higher quality leads. Someone who reads a whitepaper is more engaged than someone who just got a cold email.

**Enhancement opportunity:** Track who downloads the whitepaper and auto-enroll them in a follow-up sequence. Could also gate the download behind an email capture form for even warmer leads.

---

### Play 3: The Free Fee Analysis

**Message:** "I'll show you exactly what your retirement plan is costing you. 15 minutes, no cost."

**What it looks like:**
- Email or LinkedIn message offering a specific, tangible deliverable
- Not a vague "let's chat" -- it's "I'll pull up your plan and show you line by line what you're paying"
- The offer itself is the meeting

**Lead-in offer:** Complimentary fee analysis. Concrete, valuable, no-strings.

**Best for:** Prospects who are numbers-driven and want proof before they engage. Doctors and dentists who think in terms of ROI. Lawyers who appreciate the "fine print" angle.

**Existing assets:** Referenced in playbooks (`industry_playbooks.md`) as a core conversation starter. Mac's talk track is built around this.

**Automatable:** The outreach is automatable. The actual fee analysis requires Mac on a call, but that's the meeting itself -- which is the goal.

**Risk level:** Medium effort per lead (Mac has to actually do the analysis), but highest conversion to client because the prospect sees real numbers. This is Mac's closer.

### Play 4: The Educational Dinner Seminar

**Message:** "Come to dinner, learn something useful, no sales pitch."

**What it looks like:**
- Invite to a small-group dinner (8-12 people) at a local restaurant
- Educational topic: tax strategies, hidden fees, 2026 tax law changes
- Mac presents, answers questions, builds trust in person
- Follow-up after the event for 1-on-1 conversations

**Lead-in offer:** Free dinner + education. High-value, low-pressure.

**Best for:** Prospects who want to evaluate Mac as a person before engaging. Works especially well for dentists (study club format is already familiar) and doctors (used to CME dinner events).

**Existing assets:** Templates 1-C, 2-C, 3-C in `outreach_templates.md`. Playbook recommendations for dinner formats, venues, and topics.

**Automatable:** The invitations and follow-ups are automatable. The event itself requires Mac to show up -- but this is high-ROI effort. One dinner can yield 3-5 qualified meetings.

**Risk level:** Highest effort per event (venue, food, prep), but highest conversion rate. Mac has to be there, but a single dinner with 10 dentists is worth more than 500 cold emails.

**Enhancement opportunity:** We could handle ALL logistics (book restaurant, send invites, manage RSVPs, prep Mac's talking points, handle follow-ups). Mac's only job: show up and talk. This fits the 0% marketing effort model -- we run the event, he just attends.

### Play 5: The Social Proof / Peer Validation

**Message:** "Other [dentists/doctors/lawyers] in your area are already doing this."

**What it looks like:**
- Email that references other professionals in NE Ohio who've gone through Mac's review
- Doesn't name names (compliance), but creates peer pressure: "people like you are finding this valuable"
- Positions the meeting as something peers are doing, not a sales pitch

**Lead-in offer:** "See what your peers are discovering about their own accounts."

**Best for:** Prospects who are influenced by what colleagues are doing. Works well in tight-knit communities (dentists especially -- study clubs, dental societies).

**Existing assets:** Simpl ABI sequence Email 3 (Social Proof) in `simpl_abi_outreach_sequence.md`.

**Automatable:** Yes -- this is just a different email in the sequence. Fires on Day 8 after the initial outreach.

**Risk level:** Requires that Mac actually has some client examples to reference (even anonymously). If he's just starting out, this play is weaker until he has a few wins.

### Play 6: The Referral Network / COI Play

**Message:** (Not to prospects directly) -- "Let's partner. I send you clients, you send me clients."

**What it looks like:**
- Outreach to CPAs, estate attorneys, practice brokers, dental supply reps
- Not selling Mac's services -- building referral relationships
- Goal: get COIs to recommend Mac to their existing clients when financial planning comes up

**Lead-in offer:** Reciprocal referrals. "I'll send you business, you send me business."

**Best for:** Long game. Takes months to build but generates zero-effort leads forever once established. A single CPA who serves 50 dentists is worth more than any email campaign.

**Existing assets:** COI targets and strategies in `industry_playbooks.md` (dental CPAs, practice brokers, medical practice CPAs, estate attorneys).

**Automatable:** The initial outreach to COIs is automatable. The relationship itself is manual -- but we could automate: identifying COIs, sending intro emails, scheduling coffee/lunch meetings, and follow-up nurture sequences.

**Risk level:** Slowest to produce results, but highest long-term ROI. Zero marketing effort from Mac once relationships exist -- leads just appear.

### Play 7: The Direct Mail Piece

**Message:** Physical letter or postcard to practice address.

**What it looks like:**
- High-quality personalized letter to a practice owner
- References their specific situation (practice type, city, upcoming tax changes)
- Includes whitepaper as a physical insert or a QR code to download
- Stands out because nobody sends physical mail anymore

**Lead-in offer:** Whitepaper + meeting invitation.

**Best for:** High-value targets who are hard to reach digitally. Older professionals who still read physical mail. Dentists and doctors at practice addresses (not email-fatigued at their practice).

**Existing assets:** We'd need to create the mail piece. Content can be pulled from existing templates.

**Automatable:** Partially -- prospect list generation and mail merge are automatable. Printing and mailing requires a service (e.g., Lob.com, PostGrid) but those have APIs that can be fully automated.

**Risk level:** Higher cost per touch ($1-3/letter vs. $0 for email), but stands out in a way email never will. Best used for top-tier prospects, not bulk.

### Play 8: The LinkedIn Thought Leadership

**Message:** Not outreach -- content that attracts inbound.

**What it looks like:**
- Regular posts on Mac's LinkedIn about hidden fees, tax strategies, retirement planning myths
- Not sales posts -- educational content that positions Mac as the expert
- Comments and engagement on posts by target prospects
- Over time, prospects come to Mac instead of Mac going to them

**Lead-in offer:** No direct offer -- the content IS the marketing. Inbound inquiries come naturally.

**Best for:** Long game. Builds Mac's personal brand so when cold outreach does happen, prospects already recognize the name.

**Automatable:** Content generation is automatable (we write the posts). Publishing could be automated with scheduling tools. Engagement/commenting is harder to automate but possible with the LinkedIn co-pilot approach.

**Risk level:** Slowest to produce direct meetings, but creates a flywheel. Once Mac has 1,000+ connections and regular content, inbound leads appear without any outreach.

**Enhancement:** We write all the posts, Mac just approves (or doesn't -- we post for him). One more thing that requires 0% of his effort.

**LPL compliance:** All posts must avoid banned language. No performance claims. No return projections. Educational tone only. Mac's profile must include the LPL disclosure line.

### Play 9: LinkedIn Full-Channel Strategy (0% Mac Effort)

**Goal:** Turn Mac's LinkedIn into a lead generation engine he never touches.

#### 9A: Content Machine (We Write, We Post, We Engage)

**What it looks like:**
- We write 2-3 posts per week under Mac's name
- Educational content: hidden fees, tax strategies, retirement myths, industry-specific insights
- We post them on a schedule using a scheduling tool
- We engage on target prospects' posts (likes, comments) from Mac's account to get on their radar before cold outreach lands

**Why it works:**
- When a cold email or connection request arrives, the prospect checks Mac's LinkedIn
- If his profile has recent, relevant content, he looks credible -- not like a random cold caller
- Over time, inbound inquiries appear without any outreach
- Content also makes connection requests more likely to be accepted

**Tools:**
- Buffer or Hootsuite (free tier) -- schedule posts
- We generate all content (LPL-compliant, educational, no banned terms)
- Can also repurpose the whitepaper into a LinkedIn article series

**Automation level:** FULL. We write it, schedule it, post it. Mac never logs in.

---

#### 9B: LinkedIn Newsletter

**What it looks like:**
- We create a LinkedIn Newsletter under Mac's profile
- Monthly or biweekly issue on a rotating topic
- When someone subscribes, LinkedIn notifies them every time a new issue drops
- Subscribers grow organically as connections see the newsletter

**Why it works:**
- LinkedIn sends push notifications to all subscribers when a new issue publishes -- free distribution
- Builds an owned audience on LinkedIn (not dependent on the algorithm)
- Newsletter subscribers are warm -- they opted in. Easy to follow up with
- Content comes from what we already have: whitepaper excerpts, tax strategy breakdowns, fee analysis insights

**Example newsletter topics:**
1. "3 Fees Hiding in Your Retirement Plan Right Now"
2. "What the 2026 Tax Changes Mean for Practice Owners"
3. "The Annuity Question: Misunderstood or Misrepresented?"
4. "Cash Balance Plans: What Every High-Earning Professional Should Know"
5. "Succession Planning: Why Most Practice Sales Leave Money on the Table"

**Automation level:** FULL. We write every issue. Mac never touches it.

#### 9C: LinkedIn Events as Lead Magnets (No Live Presentation)

**What it looks like:**
- We create a LinkedIn Event that looks like a webinar but is actually a pre-recorded video or a whitepaper download
- OR: we create the event and when the "event" date arrives, attendees get a link to the whitepaper + a booking link for a 1-on-1 with Mac
- The point is NOT the event itself -- it's the RSVP list

**Why it works:**
- Creating a LinkedIn Event lets us invite Mac's entire connection list
- LinkedIn notifies connections and shows the event in their feed
- When someone RSVPs, their connections see it too (organic reach)
- We collect a list of interested people -- that's the gold
- After the event date, we follow up with every RSVP: "Thanks for your interest -- would a brief call with Bryan be worthwhile?"
- Mac never presents, never shows up, never knows it happened (until we book meetings from the RSVP list)

**Pre-recorded option:** If Mac is ever willing to record a 20-minute video once, we can replay that as the "webinar" at every event. One recording, unlimited reuse. But this is optional -- the whitepaper-download version works without any recording.

**Example events (no live component needed):**
- "Free Guide: Hidden Fees in Your Retirement Plan" (event = whitepaper download link)
- "Tax Strategies for Practice Owners — 2026 Update" (event = pre-recorded video OR whitepaper)
- "Complimentary Retirement Account Review — Reserve Your Spot" (event = booking link for 1-on-1 with Mac)

**Automation level:** FULL. We create the event, invite connections, collect RSVPs, follow up. Mac does nothing.

#### 9D: Profile Optimization (One-Time Setup)

**What it looks like:**
- Rewrite Mac's LinkedIn headline, summary, and experience to convert profile visitors into leads
- Add the whitepaper link and booking link to his profile
- Make his profile a landing page: when prospects check him out after a cold email or connection request, his profile does the selling

**Headline formula:** "[Who he helps] | [What he does] | [Credibility marker]"
Example: "Helping NE Ohio Practice Owners Reduce Retirement Fees | 20 Years on the Insurance Side | Securities offered through LPL Financial"

**Summary:** 3-4 sentences max. Pain point, what he does, soft CTA, whitepaper link.

**Automation level:** One-time setup. We do it once, done.

---

#### 9E: Engagement Farming (Warm Prospects Before Cold Outreach)

**What it looks like:**
- Before we send a connection request or cold email to a prospect, we engage with their LinkedIn content first
- Like 2-3 of their posts, leave a thoughtful comment on one
- Wait 3-5 days, then send the connection request
- The prospect recognizes Mac's name when the request arrives -- it's not truly "cold" anymore

**Why it works:**
- Dramatically increases connection acceptance rates (from ~20% cold to ~40% warm)
- The prospect feels like Mac is genuinely interested in their work, not just selling
- Sets up the follow-up message to feel natural: "Thanks for connecting -- saw your post about [topic], really resonated."

**Automation level:** HIGH. Our LinkedIn co-pilot can add an engagement step before the connection request. We identify the prospect, engage for a few days, then send the request.

**LPL compliance:** Comments must be educational/neutral. No product recommendations in public comments. No performance claims.
